## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.
When machines like ChatGPT and Voyant read literature for us, we gain speed, structure, and new ways to notice patterns that we might miss on our own. Tools like Voyant Tools help show patterns such as repetition, word frequency, structure, and what was being said before and after certain words, while ChatGPT explains meaning, themes, and rhetorical strategies in a more human-like way. Together, they make it easier to understand both the data and the message of a text. 
However, we also lose something important when we rely too much on machine reading. We can lose personal interpretation, emotional connection, and the slow thinking that comes from reading closely, which can be reduced when a tool gives us quick answers. Most literature is meant to be experienced through reading, emotions, and the human thought process, not just processed, and machines cannot fully feel tone, voice, or cultural context the way a human reader can. 
Voyant and GPT also imagine meaning differently. Voyant treats meaning as something that can be measured through patterns, repetition, and structure in the text. ChatGPT, on the other hand, treats meaning as something interpretive, shaped by themes, emotions, and rhetorical purpose. This shows that meaning in literature is both data-based and human-based, and understanding a text fully requires both approaches, similar to the way that using ChatGPT and Voyant helps, but without human understanding, it can only explain so much.
